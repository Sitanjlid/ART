ACRYLIC

Acrylic painting, technique in which pigments are mixed with hot, liquid wax. After all of the colours have been applied to the painting surface, a heating element is passed over them until the individual brush or spatula marks fuse into a uniform film.

ENCAUSTIC

The artworks in this section are strictly encaustic, meaning they are painted with pigmented wax on a substrate, such as a wood panel, and are fused or burned in through the use of a heat source, such as a heat gun or propane torch. All of the art in this section was achieved through mixing colors by applying heat to the location of the color, such as the work itself or a hot palette.

OIL PAINTING

Oil painting is a type of painting produced using oil-based paints. Oil painting involves using pigments that use a medium of drying oil as the binder and painting with them on a canvas.The most common types of oil in oil paints are linseed oil, walnut oil, poppy seed oil, and safflower oil.

IMPLASTO

Impasto is a painting technique where paint is laid on an area of the surface thickly, usually thick enough that the brush or painting-knife strokes are visible. It is a thick application of paint that does not attempt to look smooth, instead, it is proud to be textured and exists to show off brush and palette knife marks
